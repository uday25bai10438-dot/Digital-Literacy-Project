Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers try to steal sensitive
information such as passwords, bank details, or personal data by
pretending to be a trusted source. This usually happens through emails,
messages, or fake websites.

In a typical phishing attack, a user receives an email that looks like
it is from a bank or a trusted company. The email asks the user to click
on a link and enter their login details. The link redirects to a fake
website that looks real. Once the user enters their information, it is
captured by the attacker.

Students and working professionals are common targets. The consequences
include financial loss, identity theft, and data breaches.
